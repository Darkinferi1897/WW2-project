Battle of Midway (June 1942)

Location: Midway Atoll, Pacific Ocean

Often called the "turning point" of the Pacific War, U.S. Navy codebreakers intercepted Japanese plans for an ambush. The resulting carrier battle saw the U.S. sink four Japanese aircraft carriers, permanently crippling Japan’s naval offensive capabilities.

Battle of Stalingrad (1942–1943)

Location: Stalingrad, Soviet Union

One of the deadliest battles in human history. After months of brutal urban warfare in freezing conditions, Soviet forces encircled the German Sixth Army. The German surrender marked the end of their advance into the Soviet Union and began their long retreat to Berlin.

Invasion of Normandy / D-Day (June 6, 1944)

Location: Normandy, France

Codenamed "Operation Overlord," this was the largest seaborne invasion in history. Allied forces from the U.S., UK, Canada, and others stormed five beaches to liberate Western Europe from Nazi control, establishing a crucial Western Front.

Battle of Britain (1940)

Location: Skies over the United Kingdom

The first major military campaign fought entirely by air forces. The German Luftwaffe attempted to gain air superiority over the Royal Air Force (RAF) to prepare for an invasion. Britain's successful defense prevented a Nazi occupation of the UK.
