September 1, 1939

Invasion of Poland

Germany invades Poland, leading Great Britain and France to declare war on Germany two days later. World War II officially begins.

May 10, 1940

Fall of France

Germany launches an invasion of France and the Low Countries. Within weeks, the Allied forces are evacuated at Dunkirk, and France falls.

December 7, 1941

Pearl Harbor

Japan launches a surprise aerial attack on the U.S. Naval Base at Pearl Harbor, Hawaii. The United States enters the war the following day.

June 6, 1944

D-Day

Allied forces land on the beaches of Normandy, France, opening the Western Front and beginning the liberation of German-occupied Europe.

May 8, 1945

V-E Day

Victory in Europe Day. Germany signs an unconditional surrender, ending the war in the European theater.

September 2, 1945

V-J Day

Following the atomic bombings of Hiroshima and Nagasaki, Japan formally surrenders aboard the USS Missouri, ending World War II.
